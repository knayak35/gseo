A simple pathway into GSEO

From your first email to publication, then into our Research Associate track and interview team. The steps below explain what to send, what you will receive, and how to keep growing inside the community.

1

Contact GSEO

Write a short email that covers three things: your topic, the question you want to answer, and any draft or sources you already have. We will match you with an editor or peer mentor who can help refine scope and choose methods so you begin with a clear plan instead of a blank page.

  • Helpful to include: 2–4 line summary, 3–5 key sources, your preferred timeline.
  • Outcome: an initial plan with milestones and a suggested structure.
2

Submit your paper

Send a draft or full manuscript for double‑blind peer review. Editors focus on clarity, evidence, and reproducibility. You will receive line‑level comments plus a summary sheet outlining what works, what to tighten, and any missing citations or data notes.

  • What we check: research question, methodology, data integrity, figures, and citation style.
  • Outcome: a revision checklist that makes the next pass straightforward.
3

Get published

After revisions, we publish your paper in our weekly issue and assign a permanent, citable link. We also provide a compact social caption and a thumbnail so you can share your work professionally on applications and portfolios.

  • Included: citable URL, formatted PDF, and web version.
  • Outcome: your research becomes part of a searchable library used by students worldwide.
4

Research Associate

Once published, join our Article Writing Campaign under editorial heads. You will write shorter pieces on a regular cadence, help review new submissions, and support first‑time authors. This is where you develop consistency and learn how an editorial pipeline operates.

  • Cadence: weekly or bi‑weekly; we help you pick a sustainable schedule.
  • Outcome: portfolio depth, mentorship experience, and stronger editorial judgment.
5

Lead interviews

Contributors who perform well over time may be invited to lead interviews with top university students. You will prepare questions, host conversations, and distill insights for our audience. This builds communication skills and expands your academic network.

  • What we look for: reliability, clear writing, initiative, and thoughtful feedback to others.
  • Outcome: on‑camera experience and leadership within the GSEO community.
